Dear Hiring Committee,

My immersive experience caring for a loved one with dementia, coupled with a career in human services, draws me to the Dementia Program Coordinator position at CICOA.

When my grandmother's dementia diagnosis emerged after my father's passing in April 2024, the experience profoundly transformed my understanding of dementia care. What began as a temporary support visit to Chicago quickly revealed a hidden challenge: her struggles with missed appointments, financial disarray, and cognitive decline had gone unrecognized by her medical team. After reviewing her records and identifying concerning patterns, I advocated for testing, which confirmed moderate-stage dementia.

That visit evolved into nearly a year of comprehensive caregiving. Navigating unfamiliar systems with no clear starting point, I shifted immediately into problem-solving mode. After synthesizing her medical information and understanding her daily needs, I:

  • Researched best practices in dementia care, consulting medical literature and connecting with local support agencies
  • Co-developed Love in the Balance, a grant initiative supporting dementia caregivers experiencing sustained emotional stress
  • Began writing The Other Side of Dementia Caregiving: A Journey of Holding on While Letting Go, a resource blending firsthand insight with practical wellness tools for families

This turning point led to deeper engagement with care systems through research, community advocacy, and program design.

Over the past decade, I’ve led cross-sector programs that drive measurable impact. As a project manager, I launched district-wide initiatives, tracked performance metrics, secured over $500,000 in program funding, and presented outcomes to public leadership. Building on this foundation, I later served as a school principal, where I built community systems, launched trauma-informed training programs, and facilitated educational events. My leadership also included overseeing a campus food distribution network serving over 1,000 families monthly and coaching 21 aspiring educators—18 of whom transitioned into permanent teaching roles.

In my role as a Legal Fellow, I co-led trauma-informed trainings for law enforcement and authored public education tools adopted in statewide domestic violence reform. I also facilitated family support groups as an addictions counselor, supporting families navigating emotional strain and complex care systems. Collectively, these experiences reflect my ability to design, deliver, and evaluate high-impact training, volunteer coordination, and dementia-friendly outreach with precision, equity, and compassion.

What excites me most is the opportunity to bridge systems and people. Ensuring care resources are both dignified and accessible for those affected by dementia is a mission I share with CICOA. It would be an honor to bring a blend of personal insight and coordination expertise to advance dementia-friendly communities through your vital work.

Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to discussing how my background aligns with your vision.

Warmly,
Marlitha Williams-Ragland, J.D.
