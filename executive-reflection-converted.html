Executive Reflection: Designing for Literacy Impact in Unstable Conditions

At IDEA Brackenridge Academy (2022-2024), I built a resilient literacy framework that delivered measurable gains across multiple student cohorts despite extraordinary challenges:

80% of teachers working without full certification

Implementation of Texas STAAR Redesign demanding greater student adaptability
Limited bilingual staffing and significant teacher turnover

System Design & Implementation

Through comprehensive data analysis, I identified that students struggled not with decoding, but with higher-order literacy skills - vocabulary development, inferencing, and meaning-making. In response, I designed and implemented:

A vertically aligned intervention system integrating both Balanced and Structured Literacy approaches

An 80-minute Tier 3 intervention block (3x weekly) for students reading below grade level
Cross-curricular literacy integration connecting reading, writing, and science

"Screen to Scratch" - a metacognitive annotation strategy bridging digital reading with written synthesis

"Accountable Talk" routines equipping students with academic discourse structures


Measurable Results

By 2024, our literacy framework demonstrated significant impact:

Cohort C (full implementation) outperformed both district and state averages at Meets (+4%, +5%) and Masters (+3%, +4%) levels

Special Education students exceeded district averages by 22 points at Approaches, 16 at Meets, and 11 at Masters

Accelerated learners achieved a 47% passing rate, surpassing district averages by 10 points and state by 12

Emergent Bilingual students consistently outperformed district averages across all three cohorts

Teacher growth mirrored student gains: 87% improved instructional practice ratings with 9% increase in campus retention

Leadership Philosophy

My instructional leadership is anchored in three governing principles:

  1. Cognitive Clarity: Designing frameworks that address root causes rather than symptoms

  2. Instructional Adaptability: Building systems that function despite staffing instability

  3. Measurable Transfer: Ensuring skills transfer across content areas and assessment contexts

The gains we achieved weren't merely academic—they were architectural, demonstrating how thoughtful system design can produce sustainable literacy growth even under the most challenging conditions.
