Strategic Literacy Interventions: The Power of Targeted Approaches
Session Overview
This 90-minute session will help teachers understand how to strategically combine literacy components
for maximum impact, with special attention to prefix instruction as a high-leverage intervention strategy.
The session balances research insights with practical application, ensuring teachers leave with actionable
strategies they can implement immediately.
Session Objectives
Review research on the most effective intervention combinations
Analyze how targeted prefix instruction accelerates literacy development
Practice designing integrated intervention activities
Create ready-to-use intervention resources
Complete Session Plan
Opening Activity (10 minutes)
"Word Construction Challenge"
1. As teachers enter, give each a small envelope containing letter tiles or cards.
2. Instruct them to form as many words as possible in 3 minutes using only their tiles.
3. After 3 minutes, have them partner with another teacher and see how many new words they can
make by combining their tiles.
4. Debrief the activity by asking:
How did your word-building capacity change when you combined resources?
What strategies did you use when working alone versus collaboratively?
Reveal connection: "Just as combining your letter tiles created more powerful possibilities, strategically
combining literacy intervention components creates more powerful learning opportunities for our
students."
Introduction & Objectives (5 minutes)
Congratulate the team on meeting the 80% reading target
Share session objectives:
1. Review research on the most effective intervention combinations
2. Analyze how targeted prefix instruction accelerates literacy development
3. Practice designing integrated intervention activities
4. Create ready-to-use intervention resources
Research Foundation (15 minutes)
"The Science of Reading: Integration is Key"
Present key findings from the research document:
Highest effect sizes come from integrated approaches
Code-focused instruction + teacher-led delivery + writing integration = most powerful package
(ES=0.54 + 0.39 + 0.53)
The importance of sufficient duration (75-100+ sessions)
Highlight the critical "Implementation Triangle":
Knowledgeable teacher
Small group format (3-5 students)
Extended duration
Current Practice Reflection (10 minutes)
Have teachers individually reflect and note:
Which intervention components are they currently using?
How are they combining these components?
What is their typical group size and intervention duration?
Share in table groups and identify strengths and opportunities
The Power of Prefixes: Data Analysis (15 minutes)
Review the prefix data tables from the intervention resources
Small group analysis: Identify patterns in mastery levels across different prefixes
Discuss how prefix mastery connects to reading comprehension
Highlight finding: Level 2 prefixes (re-, pre-, in-, etc.) show consistent "At" mastery level while Level 3
prefixes (exo-, para-, etc.) need more attention
Strategic Intervention Design (20 minutes)
Introduce the framework for optimal prefix intervention:
1. Systematic explicit instruction
2. Reading-writing integration
3. Application to meaningful content
Model activity design using the "Triple Impact" approach:
Word level (decoding/encoding)
Sentence level (context)
Passage level (comprehension)
Collaborative Workshop (20 minutes)
In pairs, teachers select one prefix group from the resource document
Using the "Triple Impact" template, design a 15-minute intervention activity
Ensure the activity integrates:
Explicit prefix instruction
Connected writing component
Application to grade-level content
Gallery Walk & Refinement (10 minutes)
Post designed activities
Teachers use sticky notes to provide feedback and suggestions
Teams refine their activities based on feedback
Implementation Planning & Closure (10 minutes)
Individual planning: When, how, and with which students will they implement their new activities
Set a follow-up date to share implementation results
Exit ticket: One insight and one question

Implementation Summary
This PLC session follows adult learning principles by:
Starting with an engaging team-building activity (Word Construction Challenge)
Honoring teachers' existing expertise and achievements
Providing a research foundation while focusing on practical application
Including collaborative work time with peer feedback
Ensuring teachers leave with concrete, ready-to-use resources
